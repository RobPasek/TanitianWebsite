Visitor Information

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about power, money, language, safety, alcohol laws, and holidays on Taniti.

General Questions

What kind of power outlets does Taniti use?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States. Many visitors from North America can use their devices without an adapter.

What currency is used on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen, and several banks facilitate currency exchange. Most businesses accept major credit cards.

Is English widely spoken?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in Taniti City and tourist areas. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, particularly by older residents.

Alcohol & Holidays

What are the drinking laws on Taniti?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18, and the drinking age is not strictly enforced. However, alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.

Do holidays affect business hours?

Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants close on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly and check hours in advance.

Health & Safety

Is Taniti safe for visitors?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. As tourism increases, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so visitors are encouraged to keep an eye on valuables and use normal travel precautions.

What kind of medical services are available?

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees and is equipped to handle most medical needs for residents and visitors.
