Getting Here & Around

Transportation on Taniti

Learn how to arrive on Taniti and how to get around the island once you are here, using public buses, taxis, rental cars, and bikes.

Arriving on Taniti

Almost all visitors arrive by air. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. The island is in the process of expanding the airport so that larger jets will be able to land within the next few years.

Some visitors arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.

Ground Transportation

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day. Private buses provide transportation to the rest of the island.

  • Taxis are available in Taniti City.
  • Rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport.
  • Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors; helmets are required by law.

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay near Merriton Landing, which is especially easy to explore on foot.

Travel Planning Note

As the airport expands, flight options are expected to increase. For now, visitors should book flights early and confirm connections due to limited capacity.
