Visitor Services

Amenities on Taniti

From groceries and power to healthcare and safety, Taniti provides the essential services visitors need to feel comfortable during their stay.

Groceries & Everyday Needs

Taniti offers several options for buying food and basic supplies:

  • Two supermarkets.
  • Two smaller grocery stores.
  • One convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.

Money, Power, and Utilities

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks on the island facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States, so many visitors from North America can use their electronics without an adapter.

Healthcare & Safety

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics to serve both residents and visitors. The hospital employs many multilingual staff members.

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so visitors should still take normal precautions with valuables.

Language & Communication

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in Taniti City and tourist areas. In rural areas, particularly among older residents, very little English is spoken.
